## The Failure

The first version of Profita had charts. Lots of them. Stacked bar charts
for asset allocation, sparklines for performance trends, pie charts for
diversification. I was proud of the data density. Users couldn't read any
of it.

## The Pivot

We pivoted to Progressive Disclosure. Default view: one number, one trend
arrow, one short sentence. Tap to expand: more detail. Tap again: full
chart. The user controlled how much complexity they faced.

## What I Learned

Density isn't sophistication. Choosing what NOT to show is the designer's
job, not the user's.
