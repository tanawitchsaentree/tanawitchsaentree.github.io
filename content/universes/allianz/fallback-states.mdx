## The Problem

Every AI system fails. The question is: how gracefully, and who catches it?

IDAS had no principled fallback logic. When confidence was low, documents either stalled in a queue or were routed to the wrong team with false confidence.

Operators had no signal that something had gone wrong. They only discovered errors downstream.

## Key Insight

Fallback states are a UX problem, not just an engineering one. The system needs to communicate what it doesn't know, not just what it does.

Uncertainty should surface at the point of decision, not after the fact.

## What Changed

Operators gained a clear visibility tier — documents flagged for human review were separated from high-confidence automated routes. False-positive rates dropped in the first quarter post-launch.
