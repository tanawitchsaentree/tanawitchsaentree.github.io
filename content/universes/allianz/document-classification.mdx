## The problem

IDAS processed thousands of incoming documents daily — claims, invoices, legal notices. The AI classified each one and routed it to a team. When it was wrong, no one found out until something downstream broke: a claim misrouted to legal, an invoice sitting in the wrong queue for days.

When it was right, handlers still double-checked everything. Not because they distrusted the system — because the system gave them no signal about when *not* to.

The cost wasn't bad AI. It was that good AI and bad AI looked identical at the point of decision.

## The decision

The display had to answer one question for the handler: *do I need to check this, or can I trust the route?* Not a number. Not a color. A clear yes/no with the evidence underneath for the cases where it mattered.

## Trade-off ที่ยังอยู่

**Confidence thresholds are static.** Right now every document type uses the same review threshold. A legal notice and an invoice carry different downstream costs when misclassified — the threshold should reflect that, but calibrating per-type requires historical data by document category that didn't exist at launch.

**The display optimises for the handler, not the auditor.** Someone reviewing a routing decision six months later has no record of what confidence score triggered which route. There's no confidence audit trail. That's a gap that'll matter if the system is ever challenged.
